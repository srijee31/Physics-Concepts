# Waves and Sound

## Introduction

A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy from one place to another without transferring matter.

Waves are very common in nature.

Examples:

- Water waves in the ocean
- Sound waves in air
- Light waves from the Sun

---

## Types of Waves

Waves are mainly classified into two types.

### Mechanical Waves

Mechanical waves require a **medium** (such as air, water, or solids) to travel.

Examples:

- Sound waves
- Water waves

---

### Electromagnetic Waves

Electromagnetic waves do **not require a medium** to travel.

They can move through empty space.

Examples:

- Light waves
- Radio waves
- X-rays

---

## Transverse Waves

In transverse waves, the particles of the medium move **perpendicular to the direction of the wave**.

Example:

- Waves on a rope
- Water surface waves

---

## Longitudinal Waves

In longitudinal waves, the particles move **parallel to the direction of wave propagation**.

Example:

- Sound waves in air

These waves consist of:

- Compression (particles close together)
- Rarefaction (particles spread apart)

---

## Sound

Sound is a type of **mechanical wave** produced by vibrating objects.

Sound travels through a medium such as:

- Air
- Water
- Solids

Example:

A vibrating guitar string produces sound waves.

---

## Speed of Sound

The speed of sound depends on the medium through which it travels.

Approximate speeds:

- Air → 343 m/s
- Water → 1480 m/s
- Steel → 5000 m/s

Sound travels faster in **solids than in liquids or gases**.

---

## Frequency and Pitch

Frequency is the **number of vibrations per second**.

Unit of frequency: **Hertz (Hz)**

Higher frequency → higher pitch  
Lower frequency → lower pitch

Example:

A whistle produces a higher frequency sound compared to a drum.

---

## Key Points

- A wave transfers energy without transferring matter.
- Mechanical waves require a medium.
- Electromagnetic waves do not require a medium.
- Sound is a mechanical wave produced by vibrations.
- Frequency determines the pitch of sound.
