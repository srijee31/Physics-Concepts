# Speed, Velocity, and Acceleration

## Introduction

When an object moves, we often want to know how fast it is moving and how its motion changes over time.

Three important concepts used to describe motion are:

1. Speed
2. Velocity
3. Acceleration

---

## Speed

Speed is the **distance travelled by an object per unit time**.

Formula:

Speed = Distance / Time

Example:

If a car travels 100 meters in 10 seconds:

Speed = 100 / 10 = 10 m/s

Speed is a **scalar quantity** because it only has magnitude and no direction.

Examples of speed:

- 60 km/h
- 10 m/s
- 5 m/s

---

## Types of Speed

### Uniform Speed

An object is said to have uniform speed if it covers **equal distances in equal intervals of time**.

Example:

A train moving at a constant speed of 60 km/h.

### Non-Uniform Speed

An object has non-uniform speed if it covers **unequal distances in equal intervals of time**.

Example:

A car moving in city traffic.

---

## Velocity

Velocity is the **rate of change of displacement with respect to time**.

Formula:

Velocity = Displacement / Time

Velocity is a **vector quantity** because it includes both magnitude and direction.

Example:

If a car moves 20 m/s towards the east, the velocity is:

20 m/s east

---

## Difference Between Speed and Velocity

| Property | Speed | Velocity |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Type | Scalar | Vector |
| Direction | Not required | Required |
| Formula | Distance / Time | Displacement / Time |

Example:

Speed = 20 m/s  
Velocity = 20 m/s east

---

## Acceleration

Acceleration is the **rate of change of velocity with respect to time**.

Formula:

Acceleration = Change in Velocity / Time

Unit of acceleration:

m/s²

Example:

If velocity changes from 10 m/s to 20 m/s in 5 seconds:

Acceleration = (20 − 10) / 5  
Acceleration = 2 m/s²

---

## Types of Acceleration

### Positive Acceleration

When velocity increases with time.

Example:

A car speeding up.

### Negative Acceleration (Deceleration)

When velocity decreases with time.

Example:

A car slowing down when brakes are applied.

---

## Key Points

- Speed tells us how fast an object moves.
- Velocity tells us how fast and in which direction the object moves.
- Acceleration describes how velocity changes over time.
- Speed is a scalar quantity.
- Velocity and acceleration are vector quantities.
