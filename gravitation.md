# Gravitation

## Introduction

Gravitation is the force of attraction that exists between any two objects that have mass.

This force acts over a distance and pulls objects toward each other.

Example:

- The Earth pulling objects toward its surface.
- The Moon orbiting the Earth.
- Planets moving around the Sun.

---

## Universal Law of Gravitation

The law of universal gravitation was proposed by **Isaac Newton**.

Statement:

Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force that is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Formula:

F = G (m₁m₂ / r²)

Where:

- F → gravitational force
- G → gravitational constant
- m₁ → mass of first object
- m₂ → mass of second object
- r → distance between the objects

---

## Gravitational Force

Gravitational force is always:

- Attractive in nature
- Acts between all objects with mass
- Acts along the line joining the centers of the objects

---

## Acceleration Due to Gravity

When objects fall toward the Earth, they accelerate due to the Earth's gravitational pull.

This acceleration is called **acceleration due to gravity**.

Symbol: **g**

Value near Earth's surface:

g = 9.8 m/s²

---

## Weight of an Object

Weight is the force with which the Earth pulls an object toward its center.

Formula:

Weight = Mass × Gravity

W = mg

Where:

- W → weight
- m → mass
- g → acceleration due to gravity

Unit of weight: **Newton (N)**

---

## Free Fall

When an object falls under the influence of gravity alone (without any other force acting on it), the motion is called **free fall**.

Example:

- A stone dropped from a height.

---

## Key Points

- Gravitation is the force of attraction between objects with mass.
- Newton proposed the universal law of gravitation.
- Acceleration due to gravity near Earth is about **9.8 m/s²**.
- Weight is the gravitational force acting on an object.
- Objects falling under gravity alone are said to be in **free fall**.
