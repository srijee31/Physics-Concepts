# Electromagnetism

## Introduction

Electromagnetism is the branch of physics that studies the relationship between electricity and magnetism.

It explains how electric currents produce magnetic fields and how changing magnetic fields can produce electric currents.

Electromagnetism is the principle behind many electrical devices such as motors, generators, and transformers.

---

## Magnetic Field Around a Current-Carrying Conductor

When an electric current flows through a conductor, it produces a magnetic field around it.

The direction of the magnetic field can be determined using the **Right-Hand Thumb Rule**.

Right-Hand Thumb Rule:

- If the thumb of the right hand points in the direction of current
- The curled fingers show the direction of the magnetic field.

---

## Electromagnets

An electromagnet is a magnet created by passing electric current through a coil of wire.

A soft iron core is often placed inside the coil to increase the magnetic field strength.

Features of electromagnets:

- Magnetism exists only when current flows.
- The strength of the magnet can be controlled.

Examples:

- Electric bells
- Electric motors
- Magnetic cranes used to lift heavy metal objects

---

## Electromagnetic Induction

Electromagnetic induction is the process of generating electric current by changing the magnetic field around a conductor.

This principle was discovered by **Michael Faraday**.

Example:

Moving a magnet near a coil of wire can induce electric current in the wire.

---

## Electric Generator

An electric generator converts **mechanical energy into electrical energy** using electromagnetic induction.

Generators are used in:

- Power plants
- Hydroelectric stations
- Wind turbines

---

## Electric Motor

An electric motor converts **electrical energy into mechanical energy**.

It works based on the interaction between magnetic fields and electric currents.

Electric motors are used in:

- Fans
- Washing machines
- Electric vehicles
- Mixers and grinders

---

## Key Points

- Electricity and magnetism are closely related phenomena.
- Electric current produces a magnetic field.
- Changing magnetic fields can generate electric current.
- Electromagnetism is used in motors, generators, and many modern devices.
