# Wave Behaviour

## 📌 What is Wave Behaviour?

Wave behaviour describes how waves interact with surfaces, obstacles, and different media.

---

## 🔑 Main Types of Wave Behaviour

### 1. Reflection

- Bouncing back of a wave when it hits a surface

#### Laws of Reflection:
- Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection
- Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in same plane

#### Examples:
- Mirror reflection
- Echo of sound

---

### 2. Refraction

- Bending of a wave when it passes from one medium to another

#### Why it happens:
- Change in wave speed

#### Key Points:
- Frequency remains constant
- Wavelength changes
- Speed changes

#### Examples:
- Straw appears bent in water
- Lenses in glasses

---

### 3. Diffraction

- Spreading of waves when they pass through a small opening or around obstacles

#### Key Points:
- More noticeable when opening size ≈ wavelength

#### Examples:
- Sound heard around corners
- Water waves spreading through gaps

---

### 4. Interference

- When two or more waves meet and combine

#### Types:

1. Constructive Interference
   - Waves add up → larger amplitude

2. Destructive Interference
   - Waves cancel out → smaller or zero amplitude

#### Examples:
- Noise-cancelling headphones
- Light patterns (fringes)

---

## ⚡ Summary Table

| Behaviour    | What Happens              | Example                  |
|--------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| Reflection   | Wave bounces back        | Mirror, Echo            |
| Refraction   | Wave bends               | Straw in water          |
| Diffraction  | Wave spreads             | Sound around corners    |
| Interference | Waves combine            | Noise cancellation      |

---

## 🧠 Important Insight

All wave behaviours depend on:

- Wavelength
- Frequency
- Medium

---

## 🎯 Summary

Wave behaviour explains how waves interact with the world. Reflection, refraction, diffraction, and interference are fundamental concepts used in optics, sound, and electromagnetic waves.
