# 🧲 Newton’s First Law of Motion (Law of Inertia)

## 📌 What is Newton’s First Law?

A body remains:
- At **rest**, or  
- In **uniform motion (constant velocity)**  

unless acted upon by an **external unbalanced force**.

👉 In simple words:  
Objects don’t change their state on their own.

---

## 🧠 Key Idea: Inertia

👉 **Inertia = resistance to change**

Every object resists:
- Starting motion  
- Stopping motion  
- Changing direction  

---

## 🔄 Types of Inertia

### 1️⃣ Inertia of Rest
- Object at rest wants to stay at rest  

Example:
- When a bus suddenly starts, passengers fall backward  

---

### 2️⃣ Inertia of Motion
- Moving object wants to keep moving  

Example:
- When a bus stops suddenly, passengers move forward  

---

### 3️⃣ Inertia of Direction
- Object resists change in direction  

Example:
- Turning a vehicle → you feel pushed sideways  

---

## ⚖️ What is Force?

👉 Force is what changes the state of an object.

- Push or pull  
- Measured in Newton (N)  

---

## 🌍 Real-Life Examples

- Book stays on table until pushed 📚  
- Football won’t move unless kicked ⚽  
- Seatbelt protects you during sudden stop 🚗  

---

## 🎯 Why is this Important?

- Explains daily life motion  
- Foundation for Newton’s 2nd & 3rd laws  
- Used in vehicle safety, engineering, etc.  

---

## 🧠 Quick Summary

- Objects resist change → inertia  
- External force is needed to change motion  
- Three types: rest, motion, direction
