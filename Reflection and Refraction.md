# Reflection and Refraction

These concepts explain how light behaves when it hits a surface or moves from one material (like air) into another (like water).

## 1. Reflection (The "Bouncing" Effect)
Reflection is when light hits a surface and "bounces" back, just like a tennis ball bouncing off a wall.

* **The Concept:** Light cannot pass through the object, so it returns to the same side it came from.
* **The Rule:** The angle at which the light hits the surface is the exact same angle at which it bounces off.
* **Real-Life Example:** * **Mirrors:** A smooth, shiny surface reflects light perfectly so you can see your image.
    * **Rear-view Mirrors:** On your bike or car, these reflect light from traffic behind you into your eyes.



---

## 2. Refraction (The "Bending" Effect)
Refraction is the bending of light as it passes from one transparent material to another. This happens because light changes speed (it slows down or speeds up).

* **The Concept:** Imagine a car driving from a smooth road onto a sandy beach. As the wheels hit the sand, they slow down, causing the car to pull or "bend" to one side. Light does the same when moving from air into water or glass.
* **Real-Life Example:**
    * **The "Bent" Straw:** If you put a straw in a glass of water, it looks broken or shifted at the water line because the light is bending.
    * **Swimming Pools:** The bottom of a pool always looks shallower than it actually is because of refraction.
    * **Spectacles:** Lenses bend light specifically to help focus it correctly on your eyes.



---

## 3. Summary Comparison

| Feature | Reflection | Refraction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Action** | Light bounces back. | Light passes through and bends. |
| **Material** | Needs a shiny or solid surface. | Needs two different clear materials. |
| **Speed** | Light speed stays the same. | Light speed changes. |
