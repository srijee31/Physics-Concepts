# Types of Waves (Transverse & Longitudinal)

## 📌 Introduction
Waves can be classified based on how particles move relative to the direction of wave propagation.

There are two main types of waves:
1. Transverse Waves
2. Longitudinal Waves

---

## 🌊 1. Transverse Waves

In transverse waves, particles move **perpendicular** to the direction of wave propagation.

### 📍 Key Features:
- Motion of particles ⟂ direction of wave
- Has crests (peaks) and troughs (valleys)

### 📊 Examples:
- Water waves
- Light waves
- Waves on a rope

### 🧠 Visualization:
If you shake a rope up and down, the wave moves forward, but the rope moves up and down.

---

## 🔊 2. Longitudinal Waves

In longitudinal waves, particles move **parallel** to the direction of wave propagation.

### 📍 Key Features:
- Motion of particles ∥ direction of wave
- Consists of compressions and rarefactions

### 📊 Examples:
- Sound waves
- Compression waves in a spring

### 🧠 Visualization:
If you push and pull a spring, the wave travels forward, and particles move back and forth.

---

## ⚖️ Key Differences

| Feature                | Transverse Waves              | Longitudinal Waves            |
|----------------------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Particle Movement     | Perpendicular (⟂)             | Parallel (∥)                  |
| Wave Structure        | Crests & Troughs             | Compressions & Rarefactions  |
| Examples              | Light, water waves           | Sound waves                  |

---

## 🌍 Real-Life Applications
- 🎧 Sound → Longitudinal wave
- 🌊 Ocean waves → Transverse wave
- 📡 Light → Transverse electromagnetic wave

---

## 🎯 Conclusion
Understanding wave types helps in analyzing how energy travels in different mediums and is essential for studying sound, light, and electromagnetic waves.
