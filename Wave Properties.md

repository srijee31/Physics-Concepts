# Wave Properties

## Introduction
Waves exhibit certain behaviors when they interact with obstacles or different media. These behaviors are known as wave properties.

The four main properties are:
1. Reflection
2. Refraction
3. Diffraction
4. Interference

---

## 1. Reflection

### Definition
Reflection is the bouncing back of a wave when it hits a surface.

### Key Points
- Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection
- Occurs in light, sound, and water waves

### Example
- Seeing your image in a mirror
- Echo of sound

---

## 2. Refraction

### Definition
Refraction is the bending of a wave when it passes from one medium to another.

### Reason
Change in speed of the wave

### Key Points
- Speed changes → Direction changes
- Frequency remains constant
- Wavelength changes

### Example
- Straw appears bent in water
- Lenses in glasses

---

## 3. Diffraction

### Definition
Diffraction is the spreading of waves when they pass through a small opening or around obstacles.

### Key Points
- More noticeable when opening size ≈ wavelength
- Occurs in all types of waves

### Example
- Sound heard around corners
- Water waves spreading after passing through a gap

---

## 4. Interference

### Definition
Interference occurs when two or more waves overlap.

### Types

#### Constructive Interference
- Waves combine to form a larger wave
- Amplitude increases

#### Destructive Interference
- Waves cancel each other
- Amplitude decreases

### Example
- Noise cancellation headphones
- Ripple patterns in water

---

## Summary

Wave properties explain how waves behave when interacting with surroundings. These include reflection, refraction, diffraction, and interference, which are essential to understanding many real-world phenomena like vision, sound, and communication.
