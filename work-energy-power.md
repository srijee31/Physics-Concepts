# Work, Energy, and Power

## Work

In physics, work is said to be done when a **force applied on an object causes it to move in the direction of the force**.

Formula:

Work = Force × Displacement

W = F × d

Where:

- W → Work
- F → Force
- d → Displacement

SI unit of work: **Joule (J)**

### Example

If a force of **10 N** moves an object **5 meters**:

W = 10 × 5  
W = **50 Joules**

---

## Energy

Energy is the **ability to do work**.

Any object that can perform work is said to possess energy.

SI unit of energy: **Joule (J)**

### Types of Energy

#### 1. Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy is the energy possessed by an object due to its **motion**.

Formula:

KE = ½ mv²

Where:

- m → mass
- v → velocity

Example:

A moving car has kinetic energy.

---

#### 2. Potential Energy

Potential energy is the energy possessed by an object due to its **position or configuration**.

Example:

- Water stored in a dam
- A stretched spring
- An object held at a height

Gravitational potential energy formula:

PE = mgh

Where:

- m → mass
- g → acceleration due to gravity
- h → height

---

## Power

Power is the **rate at which work is done**.

Formula:

Power = Work / Time

P = W / t

Where:

- P → Power
- W → Work
- t → Time

SI unit of power: **Watt (W)**

### Example

If 100 Joules of work is done in 10 seconds:

Power = 100 / 10  
Power = **10 Watts**

---

## Key Points

- Work occurs when a force causes displacement.
- Energy is the ability to do work.
- Kinetic energy is due to motion.
- Potential energy is due to position.
- Power tells us how quickly work is done.
