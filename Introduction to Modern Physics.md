# Introduction to Modern Physics

## 1. The Photoelectric Effect
Discovered by Hertz and explained by Einstein, this proved that light behaves like a particle (photon). When light hits a material, it can eject electrons.
* **Formula:** $E = hf$ (where $h$ is Planck's constant).

## 2. Atomic Models
* **Bohr’s Model:** Electrons orbit the nucleus in specific energy levels.
* **Quantum Model:** Electrons exist in "clouds" or probabilities rather than fixed tracks.

## 3. Special Relativity (Conceptual)
Proposed by Einstein in 1905, changing our understanding of space and time:
* **Time Dilation:** Time moves slower for objects moving at very high speeds.
* **Mass-Energy Equivalence:** $E = mc^2$.

## 4. X-Rays and Radioactivity
Understanding how high-energy electromagnetic waves interact with matter, a direct evolution from the **Electromagnetic Waves** study.
