# Wave Basics: Frequency, Amplitude, Wavelength, and Period

## 1. Frequency (f)

### Definition
- **Frequency** refers to how often something happens over a certain period of time.
- For waves, it’s the number of **vibrations** or **oscillations** (complete cycles) that occur per second.
  
### Unit: Hertz (Hz)
- 1 Hz = 1 vibration per second.

### In Real Life:
- For sound, the **frequency** determines the **pitch** (high or low tone).
    - High frequency = High pitch (e.g., a whistle).
    - Low frequency = Low pitch (e.g., a drum).

### Formula for Frequency
\[
f = \frac{1}{T}
\]
Where:
- \( f \) = frequency (Hz)
- \( T \) = period (time for one complete cycle, in seconds)

---

## 2. Amplitude (A)

### Definition
- **Amplitude** is the **maximum displacement** of the wave from its **rest position**.
- It represents the **energy** of the wave.

### In Sound:
- Higher amplitude = louder sound
- Lower amplitude = softer sound

---

## 3. Wavelength (λ)

### Definition
- **Wavelength** is the **distance** between two consecutive points that are in phase (e.g., from peak to peak or trough to trough).
- For sound waves, it’s the **distance** between compressions or rarefactions.

### In Sound:
- Longer wavelength = lower frequency (lower pitch)
- Shorter wavelength = higher frequency (higher pitch)

---

## 4. Period (T)

### Definition
- **Period** is the time it takes for one complete cycle of the wave (from one point to the next same point, like from one peak to the next peak).

### Formula for Period:
\[
T = \frac{1}{f}
\]
Where:
- \( T \) = period (seconds)
- \( f \) = frequency (Hz)

---

## Example:

If you have a sound wave with a frequency of **440 Hz** (which is the pitch of **A4** on a piano):
- That means 440 complete oscillations happen in **one second**.
- If the wave’s **amplitude** is high, the sound will be loud.
- If the **wavelength** is short, it will have a higher pitch (like a whistle).
- The **period** will be \( 1/440 \) seconds or about **0.00227 seconds**.

---

## Summary
These fundamental properties — **Frequency**, **Amplitude**, **Wavelength**, and **Period** — are key to understanding how waves behave, especially in sound, light, and other wave phenomena. Mastering these will help you dive deeper into wave behaviors, like **Doppler Effect** and more advanced topics.
