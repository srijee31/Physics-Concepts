# Oscillations and Waves (Basics)

## 📌 Introduction
Oscillations and waves are fundamental concepts in physics that describe how objects move and how energy travels.

---

## 🔁 1. What is Oscillation?
Oscillation is a repeated back-and-forth motion of an object around a central position.

### Examples:
- Pendulum motion
- Spring motion
- Vibrating guitar string

---

## 🌊 2. What is a Wave?
A wave is a way in which energy travels from one place to another without the movement of matter.

### Examples:
- Water waves
- Sound waves
- Light waves

---

## 📏 3. Amplitude
Amplitude is the maximum displacement of a particle from its equilibrium (mean) position.

### Key Points:
- Higher amplitude → more energy
- Determines loudness (in sound) or brightness (in light)

---

## ⏱️ 4. Frequency
Frequency is the number of oscillations (or waves) produced per second.

### Formula:
f = Number of oscillations / Time

### Unit:
Hertz (Hz)

### Key Points:
- Higher frequency → higher pitch (in sound)

---

## 🔄 5. Time Period
Time period is the time taken to complete one full oscillation.

### Formula:
T = 1 / f

Where:
- T = Time period
- f = Frequency

---

## 📡 6. Wavelength
Wavelength is the distance between two consecutive points in the same phase (like crest to crest or trough to trough).

### Symbol:
λ (lambda)

---

## ⚡ 7. Wave Speed
Wave speed is the speed at which the wave travels.

### Formula:
v = f × λ

Where:
- v = wave speed
- f = frequency
- λ = wavelength

---

## 🌍 Real-Life Applications
- 🎧 Music → Frequency determines pitch
- 🔊 Loudness → Depends on amplitude
- 🌊 Ocean waves → Show wavelength and speed

---

## 🎯 Conclusion
Understanding oscillations and waves is essential because it forms the foundation for:
- Sound waves
- Light waves
- Electromagnetic waves
