# 📊 Graphs of Motion

## 📌 What are Graphs of Motion?

Graphs of motion help us **visually understand how an object moves over time**.

👉 Two important graphs:
1. Distance-Time Graph  
2. Velocity-Time Graph  

---

# 📈 1. Distance-Time Graph

## 🧠 What it shows?

- Distance on Y-axis  
- Time on X-axis  

👉 It shows how distance changes with time.

---

## 📊 Types of Distance-Time Graphs

### 1️⃣ Straight Line (Constant Speed)

- Distance increases uniformly  
- Object moves with constant speed  

👉 Graph: straight line

---

### 2️⃣ Curved Line (Changing Speed)

- Distance increases unevenly  
- Speed is changing  

👉 Graph: curve

---

### 3️⃣ Horizontal Line (At Rest)

- Distance does not change  
- Object is not moving  

👉 Graph: flat line

---

## 📌 Important Concept

👉 **Slope of Distance-Time Graph = Speed**

---

# 📉 2. Velocity-Time Graph

## 🧠 What it shows?

- Velocity on Y-axis  
- Time on X-axis  

👉 It shows how velocity changes with time.

---

## 📊 Types of Velocity-Time Graphs

### 1️⃣ Straight Line Upwards (Uniform Acceleration)

- Velocity increases steadily  
- Constant acceleration  

---

### 2️⃣ Straight Line Downwards (Uniform Deceleration)

- Velocity decreases steadily  

---

### 3️⃣ Horizontal Line (Constant Velocity)

- No acceleration  
- Velocity is constant  

---

### 4️⃣ Curve (Non-uniform Acceleration)

- Acceleration is changing  

---

## 📌 Important Concepts

👉 **Slope of Velocity-Time Graph = Acceleration**  

👉 **Area under Velocity-Time Graph = Displacement**

---

## ⚠️ Key Differences

| Feature | Distance-Time Graph | Velocity-Time Graph |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Slope | Speed | Acceleration |
| Area | No meaning | Displacement |
| Shape | Straight/Curve | Straight/Curve |

---

## 🌍 Real-Life Understanding

- Bike moving at constant speed → straight line  
- Car accelerating → upward sloping line  
- Braking → downward slope  
- Standing still → flat line  

---

## 🎯 Quick Summary

- Distance-Time → slope gives speed  
- Velocity-Time → slope gives acceleration  
- Area under v-t graph = displacement
