# Electricity

## Introduction

Electricity is the flow of electric charge through a conductor.

It plays an important role in modern life and powers many devices such as lights, computers, and machines.

Electric current is usually carried by **electrons moving through a conductor** like copper wire.

---

## Electric Charge

Electric charge is a fundamental property of matter.

There are two types of electric charges:

- Positive charge
- Negative charge

Important rule:

Like charges repel each other, while unlike charges attract each other.

Unit of electric charge: **Coulomb (C)**

---

## Electric Current

Electric current is the **flow of electric charge through a conductor**.

Formula:

Current = Charge / Time

I = Q / t

Where:

- I → Electric current
- Q → Charge
- t → Time

SI unit of electric current: **Ampere (A)**

---

## Electric Potential Difference (Voltage)

Voltage is the **difference in electric potential between two points in a circuit**.

It is the force that pushes electric charges through a conductor.

Unit of voltage: **Volt (V)**

Example:

A battery provides the voltage needed to move electrons through a circuit.

---

## Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance.

Formula:

V = I × R

Where:

- V → Voltage
- I → Current
- R → Resistance

Unit of resistance: **Ohm (Ω)**

---

## Electric Power

Electric power is the rate at which electrical energy is used or converted.

Formula:

P = V × I

Where:

- P → Power
- V → Voltage
- I → Current

SI unit of electric power: **Watt (W)**

Example:

A 60 W bulb consumes electrical energy at a rate of 60 watts.

---

## Key Points

- Electricity is the flow of electric charges.
- Electric current is measured in amperes.
- Voltage is the force that drives electric current.
- Ohm’s law relates voltage, current, and resistance.
- Electric power is measured in watts.
