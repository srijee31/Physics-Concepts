# Motion

## Introduction

Motion is one of the most fundamental concepts in physics.

A body is said to be in **motion** if its position changes with respect to time when compared to a reference point.

Example:

If a car moves from one place to another, its position changes over time.  
Therefore, the car is in motion.

---

## Reference Point

To determine motion, we always need a **reference point**.

A reference point is a fixed object or position used to determine whether something is moving or not.

Example:

If you are sitting in a bus:

- To a person inside the bus → you appear at rest.
- To a person standing on the road → you are moving.

So motion depends on the **frame of reference**.

---

## Types of Motion

Motion can occur in different ways.

### 1. Linear Motion

Linear motion occurs when an object moves in a straight line.

Examples:

- A car moving on a straight road
- A train moving on a straight track

---

### 2. Circular Motion

Circular motion occurs when an object moves along a circular path.

Examples:

- A rotating fan
- The motion of planets around the sun

---

### 3. Oscillatory Motion

Oscillatory motion occurs when an object moves back and forth about a fixed position.

Examples:

- A swinging pendulum
- A vibrating guitar string

---

### 4. Rotational Motion

Rotational motion occurs when an object rotates around an axis.

Examples:

- Earth rotating on its axis
- A spinning wheel

---

## Distance and Displacement

### Distance

Distance is the **total path length** travelled by an object.

It is a **scalar quantity**.

Example:

If a person walks 5 meters forward and then 5 meters backward:

Distance = 10 meters

---

### Displacement

Displacement is the **shortest straight-line distance** between the initial and final positions.

It is a **vector quantity** because it includes direction.

Example:

If a person walks 5 meters forward and then returns to the starting point:

Displacement = 0 meters

---

## Key Points

- Motion means change in position with respect to time.
- Motion always requires a **reference point**.
- Motion can be linear, circular, oscillatory, or rotational.
- Distance is a **scalar quantity**.
- Displacement is a **vector quantity**.
