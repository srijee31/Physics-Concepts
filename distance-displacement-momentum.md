# Distance, Displacement, and Momentum

## Distance

Distance is the **total path length travelled by an object**, regardless of direction.

Distance only tells us **how much ground has been covered**.

Distance is a **scalar quantity** because it has only magnitude.

### Example

If a person walks:

- 5 meters forward
- then 3 meters backward

Distance = 5 + 3 = **8 meters**

---

## Displacement

Displacement is the **shortest straight-line distance between the initial and final position of an object**.

Displacement also includes **direction**, so it is a **vector quantity**.

### Example

If a person walks:

- 5 meters forward
- then 3 meters backward

Displacement = **2 meters forward**

---

## Difference Between Distance and Displacement

| Property | Distance | Displacement |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Type | Scalar | Vector |
| Direction | Not required | Required |
| Path | Actual path travelled | Shortest path |

Example:

If a person walks around a circular track and returns to the starting point:

Distance = Total length of the track  
Displacement = 0

---

## Momentum

Momentum describes the **quantity of motion** an object has.

It depends on **mass** and **velocity**.

### Formula

Momentum = Mass × Velocity

p = m × v

Where:

- p → momentum
- m → mass
- v → velocity

The SI unit of momentum is:

kg·m/s

---

## Example of Momentum

If a ball of mass **2 kg** moves with a velocity of **5 m/s**:

p = m × v  
p = 2 × 5  
p = **10 kg·m/s**

This means the moving ball carries momentum.

---

## Key Points

- Distance is the total path travelled by an object.
- Displacement is the shortest distance between initial and final positions.
- Distance is a scalar quantity.
- Displacement is a vector quantity.
- Momentum is the product of mass and velocity.
