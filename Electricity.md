# ⚡ What is Electricity?

## 📌 Simple Definition

Electricity is the flow of tiny charged particles called **electrons** through a material (like a wire).

---

## 🧠 Easy Analogy

Think of electricity like water flowing in a pipe:

- Electrons → water  
- Wire → pipe  
- Flow → current  

If water flows → electricity flows

---

## 🔌 Where does Electricity come from?

- Battery 🔋  
- Power station ⚡  
- Generator  

These sources **push electrons to move**

---

## ⚡ What is Electric Current?

Electric current is the **flow of electrons**.

- More flow → more current  
- Less flow → less current  

Unit: Ampere (A)

---

## 👋 What happens when you TOUCH electricity?

### ⚠️ Case 1: Static Electricity (Small Shock)

Example:
- Touching a door and feeling a small spark  

- Harmless  
- Caused by charge buildup and sudden release  

---

### ⚠️ Case 2: Electric Shock (Dangerous)

When you touch a live wire:

- Your body becomes a path for current  
- Electricity flows through your body to the ground  

---

## 😵 Effects on Body

- Tingling sensation  
- Muscle contraction  
- Pain or shock  
- In severe cases → dangerous  

---

## 🧠 Why does it hurt?

Electricity interferes with:

- Nerves  
- Muscles  
- Heart signals  

---

## 🔥 Key Points

- Electricity = moving electrons  
- Shock = current passing through your body  
- Wet body → more dangerous  
- High voltage → more dangerous  

---

## 🎯 Summary

Electricity is the movement of electrons. When this flow passes through your body, it causes an electric shock, which can be mild or dangerous depending on the amount of current.
