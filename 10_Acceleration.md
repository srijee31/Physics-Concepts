
# 🚀 Acceleration

## 📌 What is Acceleration?

Acceleration is the **rate of change of velocity with respect to time**.

👉 In simple words:  
How fast velocity is changing.

- If speed increases → acceleration  
- If speed decreases → deceleration (negative acceleration)  
- If direction changes → also acceleration (even if speed is constant)

---

## 📏 Formula

a = (v - u) / t

Where:
- a = Acceleration (m/s²)
- v = Final velocity
- u = Initial velocity
- t = Time taken

---

## 📐 Unit of Acceleration

SI Unit: **m/s² (meter per second squared)**

---

## 🔄 Types of Acceleration

### 1. Uniform Acceleration
- Velocity changes at a constant rate
- Example: Free fall under gravity

### 2. Non-Uniform Acceleration
- Velocity changes at different rates
- Example: Car in traffic

---

## 🔻 Deceleration (Negative Acceleration)

When velocity decreases, acceleration is negative.

Example:
- Applying brakes on a bike 🛵

---

## 🧠 Important Points

- Acceleration depends on **change in velocity**, not just speed  
- Change in direction = acceleration (even at constant speed)  
- Zero acceleration means velocity is constant  

---

## 🌍 Real-Life Examples

- A car speeding up 🚗  
- A bike slowing down 🛵  
- A ball falling from height ⚽  
- Circular motion (direction keeps changing)  

---

## 🎯 Quick Summary

- Acceleration = change in velocity over time  
- Formula: a = (v - u) / t  
- Unit: m/s²  
- Can be positive, negative, or zero
