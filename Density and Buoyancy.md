# Density and Buoyancy

These concepts explain why some objects float while others sink. It all depends on how "packed" an object is and how the liquid pushes back.

## 1. Density (How "Packed" is it?)
Density is a measure of how much mass is crammed into a specific volume.

* **The Concept:** Think of two identical boxes. One is filled with **cotton** and the other with **iron**. The iron box is much heavier because it is more "dense."
* **The Formula:**
  $$\rho = \frac{m}{V}$$
  *(Where $\rho$ is density, $m$ is mass, and $V$ is volume).*
* **Rule of Thumb:** If an object is denser than water, it sinks. If it is less dense, it floats.



---

## 2. Buoyancy (The "Upward Push")
Buoyancy is the upward force that a fluid (like water or air) exerts on an object placed in it.

* **The Concept:** When you try to push an empty plastic bottle underwater, you feel the water pushing it back up. That "push" is the buoyant force.
* **Archimedes' Principle:** The upward buoyant force is equal to the weight of the fluid that the object displaces (pushes out of the way).
* **Real-Life Example:**
    * **Ships:** Even though ships are made of heavy steel, they are hollow and filled with air. This makes their *average* density lower than water, so they float.
    * **Life Jackets:** They are filled with very light materials (like foam) to decrease your overall density, keeping you above the water.

## 3. Summary Table

| Term | Simple Definition | Real-Life Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Density** | How heavy something is for its size. | A lead ball vs. a tennis ball. |
| **Buoyancy** | The upward "lift" from water or air. | Why you feel lighter in a swimming pool. |
| **Sinking** | Density of object > Density of fluid. | Dropping a coin in a fountain. |
| **Floating** | Density of object < Density of fluid. | An ice cube in a glass of water. |
