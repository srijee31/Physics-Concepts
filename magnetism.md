# Magnetism

## Introduction

Magnetism is a physical phenomenon produced by the motion of electric charges.  
It results in attractive and repulsive forces between objects.

Materials that produce magnetic fields are called **magnets**.

Common examples include:

- Bar magnets
- Horseshoe magnets
- The Earth itself behaves like a giant magnet.

---

## Magnetic Poles

Every magnet has two poles:

- North Pole
- South Pole

Important rules:

- Like poles repel each other.
- Unlike poles attract each other.

Example:

North pole of one magnet attracts the south pole of another magnet.

---

## Magnetic Field

A magnetic field is the region around a magnet where magnetic forces can be observed.

Magnetic fields are often represented by **magnetic field lines**.

Properties of magnetic field lines:

- They emerge from the north pole.
- They enter the south pole.
- They never intersect each other.

---

## Magnetic Materials

Materials respond differently to magnetic fields.

### Magnetic Materials

These materials are strongly attracted by magnets.

Examples:

- Iron
- Nickel
- Cobalt

---

### Non-Magnetic Materials

These materials are not attracted by magnets.

Examples:

- Wood
- Plastic
- Glass

---

## Earth's Magnetism

The Earth behaves like a **giant magnet**.

It has a magnetic field that helps in navigation.

A compass works because its needle aligns with the Earth's magnetic field.

---

## Uses of Magnets

Magnets are used in many devices:

- Electric motors
- Loudspeakers
- Magnetic storage devices
- Compasses

---

## Key Points

- Magnetism arises from moving electric charges.
- Every magnet has a north pole and a south pole.
- Like poles repel and unlike poles attract.
- Magnetic fields exist around magnets.
- The Earth itself behaves like a giant magnet.
