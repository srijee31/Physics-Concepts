# Sound Waves

## Introduction
Sound waves are a type of mechanical wave that require a medium (solid, liquid, or gas) to travel. They are produced by vibrating objects.

Sound cannot travel through a vacuum.

---

## Nature of Sound Waves

- Sound waves are **longitudinal waves**
- Particles of the medium vibrate parallel to the direction of wave propagation

### Key Terms

- Compression → Region where particles are close together
- Rarefaction → Region where particles are spread apart

---

## Characteristics of Sound Waves

### 1. Frequency (f)
- Number of vibrations per second
- Unit: Hertz (Hz)
- Determines pitch (high or low sound)

### 2. Wavelength (λ)
- Distance between two compressions or rarefactions

### 3. Time Period (T)
- Time taken for one complete vibration

### 4. Amplitude
- Maximum displacement of particles
- Determines loudness

---

## Speed of Sound

Speed depends on the medium:

- Fastest in solids
- Slower in liquids
- Slowest in gases

### Formula

v = f × λ

Where:
- v = speed of sound
- f = frequency
- λ = wavelength

---

## Factors Affecting Speed

- Temperature → Higher temperature = higher speed
- Medium density → Denser medium = faster sound

---

## Types of Sound Based on Frequency

1. Infrasonic (< 20 Hz)
2. Audible (20 Hz – 20,000 Hz)
3. Ultrasonic (> 20,000 Hz)

---

## Applications of Sound Waves

- SONAR (used in submarines)
- Medical ultrasound scanning
- Communication (speech, phones)
- Music and entertainment

---

## Summary

Sound waves are mechanical longitudinal waves that require a medium to travel. Their behavior depends on properties like frequency, wavelength, and amplitude, and they are widely used in technology and daily life.
