# Semiconductor Electronics

## 1. Introduction
Semiconductors are materials with electrical conductivity between a conductor (like copper) and an insulator (like glass). They are the foundation of all modern electronics.

## 2. Energy Bands
* **Valence Band:** The energy level where electrons are bound to atoms.
* **Conduction Band:** The energy level where electrons can move freely to conduct electricity.
* **Energy Gap ($E_g$):** The "forbidden" gap between these two bands. In semiconductors, this gap is small enough for electrons to jump across when excited.

## 3. Types of Semiconductors
* **Intrinsic:** Pure semiconductor material (e.g., Silicon, Germanium).
* **Extrinsic:** Doped with impurities to increase conductivity.
    * **n-type:** Doped with pentavalent atoms (extra electrons).
    * **p-type:** Doped with trivalent atoms (creates "holes").

## 4. The P-N Junction
When a p-type and n-type material are joined, they create a **Depletion Region**. This is the basis for:
* **Diodes:** Allow current to flow in only one direction.
* **Transistors:** Act as switches or amplifiers (the "brain" of a CPU).

## 5. Practical Application
Every microchip in a **Pixel Watch** or smartphone relies on billions of transistors to process logic gates (0s and 1s).
