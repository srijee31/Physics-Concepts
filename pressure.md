# Pressure

## Introduction

Pressure is defined as the **force applied per unit area**.

When a force acts on a surface, the effect of that force depends on how much area it is applied over.

Formula:

Pressure = Force / Area

P = F / A

Where:

- P → Pressure
- F → Force
- A → Area

SI unit of pressure: **Pascal (Pa)**

1 Pascal = 1 Newton per square meter.

---

## Example of Pressure

If a force of **10 N** is applied on an area of **2 m²**:

P = 10 / 2  
P = **5 Pa**

This means the pressure on the surface is 5 Pascal.

---

## Effects of Pressure

Pressure depends on two factors:

1. Force applied
2. Area over which the force acts

### Increasing Pressure

Pressure increases when:

- Force increases
- Area decreases

Example:

A **sharp knife** cuts easily because the area of the edge is very small, producing high pressure.

---

### Decreasing Pressure

Pressure decreases when:

- Area increases

Example:

**Camels have broad feet**, which reduce pressure on sand and prevent them from sinking.

---

## Pressure in Liquids

Liquids also exert pressure.

Liquid pressure depends on:

- Depth of the liquid
- Density of the liquid
- Gravitational acceleration

Important observations:

- Pressure increases with depth.
- Pressure acts in all directions.

Example:

Divers experience greater pressure when they go deeper into water.

---

## Atmospheric Pressure

The air surrounding the Earth exerts pressure on all objects.

This pressure is called **atmospheric pressure**.

Average atmospheric pressure at sea level is approximately:

101,325 Pa

Example:

Drinking through a straw works due to atmospheric pressure.

---

## Key Points

- Pressure is force per unit area.
- SI unit of pressure is Pascal (Pa).
- Pressure increases when force increases or area decreases.
- Liquids exert pressure that increases with depth.
- Air around us exerts atmospheric pressure.
