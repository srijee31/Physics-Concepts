# 🌊 Fluids and Buoyancy

## 1. Introduction to Fluids

A **fluid** is a substance that has the ability to flow. This includes:

- **Liquids** (e.g., water, oil)
- **Gases** (e.g., air, helium)

In physics, fluids are studied in terms of **pressure**, **density**, and **flow**.

---

## 2. Properties of Fluids

### 2.1 **Density**
- **Density** is the mass of a substance per unit volume.

### 2.3 **Viscosity**
- **Viscosity** is a measure of a fluid's resistance to flow.

Example:
- **Water** has low viscosity, so it flows easily.
- **Honey** has high viscosity, so it flows slowly.

## 3. Buoyancy

**Buoyancy** is the upward force exerted by a fluid on an object submerged in it. This force is equal to the **weight of the fluid displaced by the object**.

### 3.1 **Archimedes’ Principle**

Archimedes' principle states:

> **An object submerged in a fluid experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the object.**

Formula for buoyant force:
F_b = \rho_{fluid} \times V_{displaced} \times g

Where:
- F_b = buoyant force (N)
- ρ = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- V = volume of the fluid displaced (m³)
- g = acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²)

---

### 3.2 **Floating and Sinking**

Whether an object floats or sinks in a fluid depends on its **density relative to the fluid’s density**.

#### **Floating Object:**
An object floats if its **density is less than the fluid’s density**. It displaces a volume of fluid whose weight is equal to the object’s weight.

Example:
- **Ice floats on water** because ice is less dense than water.

#### **Sinking Object:**
An object sinks if its **density is greater than the fluid’s density**.

Example:
- **A rock sinks in water** because the rock is denser than water.

---

## 4. Real-Life Example: Boat Floating on Water

Let’s say you have a **boat** floating on water.

- The boat displaces water, and the **buoyant force** pushing up on the boat is equal to the weight of the water displaced.
- The boat stays afloat as long as the **boat’s weight** is equal to the **buoyant force**.

### How much water does the boat displace?
The amount of water displaced depends on the **volume** of the boat submerged in the water.

---

## 5. How Pressure Changes with Depth in Fluids

In a fluid at rest, pressure increases with depth.

Formula for pressure at a depth **h**:
P = \rho \times g \times h

- g = acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²)
- h = depth (m)

---

## 6. Simple Summary

- **Fluids** include liquids and gases that flow and have measurable properties like **density**, **pressure**, and **viscosity**.
- **Buoyancy** is the upward force exerted by a fluid, causing objects to float or sink based on their density.
- **Archimedes' principle** helps explain why ships and icebergs float, even though they are much denser than water.
