# Superposition Principle

## 📌 What is Superposition?

The superposition principle states:

When two or more waves overlap, the resultant displacement at any point is the sum of the individual displacements.

---

## 🔑 Simple Meaning

- Waves don’t destroy each other permanently
- They just **add up temporarily** when they meet

---

## 🧠 Mathematical Representation

Resultant displacement:

y = y₁ + y₂

Where:
- y = total displacement
- y₁, y₂ = individual wave displacements

---

## 🌊 Types of Superposition

### 1. Constructive Superposition

- Waves are in the same phase
- Amplitudes add up

Result:
- Bigger wave
- More energy

---

### 2. Destructive Superposition

- Waves are in opposite phase
- Amplitudes cancel each other

Result:
- Smaller or zero wave

---

## ⚡ Key Conditions

- Waves must overlap in space and time
- Medium should allow wave propagation

---

## 🎧 Real-Life Examples

- Noise cancelling headphones
- Sound systems (bass boost / cancellation)
- Light interference patterns

---

## 🔥 Important Insight

Superposition is the **base concept behind interference**.

Without this, interference doesn’t exist.

---

## 🎯 Summary

Superposition explains how multiple waves combine to form a new wave. It leads to constructive and destructive interference depending on phase differences.
